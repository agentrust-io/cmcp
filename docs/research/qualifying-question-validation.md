# Qualifying Question Validation Plan

**The question:** "What business are you giving up today because you cannot prove data stays protected during processing?"

**Hypothesis:** This question surfaces concrete urgency (a named lost deal, a delayed production launch, a specific compliance deadline) consistently enough to anchor the sales motion for production-bound regulated buyers.

**Why validate it:** The regulated buyer GTM motion leads with this question. If it does not surface concrete urgency within the first two responses, the entry point is wrong and needs adjustment before CC Summit.

**Timeline:** Results needed before June 23 (CC Summit).

---

## Test 1 — LinkedIn Poll

**Purpose:** Quantitative signal on whether the proof gap resonates as a live concern, not just a theoretical one.

**Poll design:**

Question: "What is your biggest blocker to moving AI agents to production?"
- Compliance team cannot approve what they cannot verify [target response]
- Model accuracy and reliability
- Integration complexity with existing systems
- Cost and infrastructure
- Other (comment)

**Targeting:** AI practitioners and decision-makers in regulated industries (financial services, healthcare, legal, insurance). Minimum 200 impressions, target 50+ responses.

**Success threshold:** >25% select "Compliance team cannot approve what they cannot verify." This confirms the proof gap is a live concern in the target segment, not just a problem we have identified theoretically.

**If it fails (< 10%):** The framing may be too abstract. Test an alternative: "Have you delayed an AI production launch because of compliance or audit requirements?" (yes/no) — this tests the lived experience rather than the framing.

---

## Test 2 — Embedded Sales Conversation Test

**Purpose:** Validate that the qualifying question surfaces a specific, named situation rather than a generic "yes that's a concern."

**Protocol:** Embed the exact qualifying question verbatim in the first 5 enterprise conversations with AI platform leads or CISOs in regulated industries:

"Before we get into what we're building — let me ask you one question. What business are you giving up today because you cannot prove data stays protected during processing?"

**What to track for each conversation:**
- Did the prospect pause (signals the question landed)?
- Did the prospect give a specific example (named deal, named auditor, named regulation) or a generic answer?
- Did the prospect redirect to a different problem (signals wrong entry point for this prospect)?
- How many turns before the conversation reached concrete urgency?

**Success threshold:** 3 of 5 conversations produce a specific, named example within 2 conversational turns. The prospect names a lost deal, a delayed deployment, an upcoming audit, or a specific customer who has pushed back.

**If it fails:** The question is too abstract or too far ahead of where the prospect is. Test alternative framings:
- "Has a compliance team or auditor ever asked you to prove something about how your AI handled data, not just describe your policies?" (past-tense, lower stakes)
- "Which of your customers is most likely to ask you how their data was handled by your agents — and what would you tell them?" (personalized, specific)

---

## Test 3 — A/B Framing Test

**Framing A (current):** "What business are you giving up today because you cannot prove data stays protected during processing?"

**Framing B (alternative, lower stakes):** "The next time a regulator or enterprise customer asks how your AI handled their data on a specific date, what evidence do you hand them?"

Framing A targets urgency (current loss). Framing B targets anticipation (future requirement). Both can open the proof gap conversation.

**Run each framing in 5 conversations (total 10). Compare:**
- Concrete specificity of response (specific vs. generic)
- Emotional engagement (did the prospect visibly react?)
- Time to qualification (how many turns to reach concrete urgency?)
- Conversion to a next meeting

**Recommendation:** Use whichever framing produces higher specificity and shorter time to qualification. If both perform similarly, use Framing A (the urgency framing) for production-bound regulated buyers and Framing B (the anticipation framing) for early-stage buyers.

---

## Output and Decision

**Go signal:** Framing A or B surfaces a specific, named situation in 3+ of 5 conversations AND LinkedIn poll shows >25% response on the compliance blocker option. Proceed with this qualifying question as the regulated buyer entry point.

**Adjust signal:** Qualifying question surfaces generic concern but not specific situations. Adjust to the anticipation framing (Framing B) or add a follow-up: "Can you tell me about a specific situation where that was a problem?"

**Pivot signal:** Question consistently redirects to other problems (model quality, integration, cost). The regulated buyer segment is not the primary early adopter. Revisit segment targeting before CC Summit.

## Integration with CC Summit Launch

Results from Tests 1 and 2 must be available by June 20 (3 days before CC Summit). If Tests 1 and 2 are still running at that point, use whatever signal exists plus the A/B test data from conversations to date. Do not launch CC Summit without at least 5 qualifying question conversations completed and analyzed.
